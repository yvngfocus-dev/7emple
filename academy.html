Track 01
Foundation
Brand Content Strategy
+
Why this track exists

Most people start with content and never build a strategy. They post because they know they should, pick topics randomly and burn out within three months wondering why nothing worked. This track fixes the foundation before you film a single frame.

The brands that consistently grow aren't posting more — they're posting with a clearer point of view. This track gives you that: a positioning you own, content pillars that never run dry and a voice that makes people stop scrolling.

Modules
  • Positioning: finding the gap only you can own
  • Audience mapping — who actually buys
  • Content pillar framework (the 3+1 system)
  • Brand voice: writing like a person, not a company
  • Building a content calendar that doesn't break
  • Measuring what matters (not vanity metrics)
  • The 90-day brand sprint — from zero to consistent
Track 02
Production
Videography for Brands
+
Why this track exists

Video is the highest-converting content format by a large margin — and the gap between "I should be doing video" and actually doing it is almost always fear, not ability. The camera isn't the problem. Not knowing what to shoot, how to structure it and how to make it feel professional is.

This track is for brands who need to be producing video but don't have a full production crew. We teach the principles — light, movement, pacing, story — that make the difference between something that looks cheap and something that builds trust.

Modules
  • The one-person video setup that looks like a team
  • Lighting on any budget
  • Scripting brand films and VSLs that convert
  • Short-form vs long-form — when to use what
  • Editing rhythm: why pacing makes or breaks it
  • Colour grading for brand consistency
  • The aftermovie formula — turning events into growth
Track 03
Personal Brand
The Personal Brand Playbook
+
Why this track exists

The most underpriced asset most coaches, trainers and consultants have is themselves. People don't hire the best service — they hire the person they trust most. A personal brand isn't vanity. It's leverage.

Every case study we've built at 7EMPLE that produced the biggest results — Strength 2 Strength, Nino Athletics, Sem Koopman, VitaQ — was built around a person first, a service second. This track teaches that system.

  • Content that attracts clients vs content that gets likes

  • The rate-raising roadmap

Track 04
Growth
Social Media That Converts
+
Why this track exists

Follower count is not a business metric. Revenue is. There are accounts with 200K followers who make nothing and accounts with 4K followers who are fully booked six months out. The difference is a conversion system, not a content calendar.

This track is about closing the gap between your audience and your clients — the DM strategy, the content-to-call pipeline, the trust-building cadence that turns someone who found you on Instagram into someone who pays you without hesitation.

Modules
  • The algorithm is not your problem (your content is)
  • Hook engineering — the first 2 seconds that determine everything
  • The trust ladder: from stranger to client in 5 posts
  • DM strategy that doesn't feel like cold outreach
  • Repurposing one piece of content across 5 platforms
  • Engagement that actually builds community
  • When and how to run paid amplification
Track 05
Advanced
Sponsorship & Brand Deals
+
Why this track exists

Sponsorships are the ultimate proof of brand equity — someone paying you because of the audience and trust you've built, not just for a deliverable. Most people assume this requires hundreds of thousands of followers. It doesn't.

The Grizzlies secured sponsorships from three countries off the back of a single aftermovie and a built brand presence — at €0 in ad spend. This track teaches the media kit, the outreach, the negotiation and the positioning that makes brands want to be associated with you.

Modules
  • What sponsors actually look for (it's not follower count)
  • Building a media kit that commands a response
  • The cold outreach template that actually works
  • Pricing your sponsorship packages
  • Delivering sponsorship content that gets renewed
  • From one sponsor to a portfolio
  • Case study: Grizzlies Zoetermeer (full breakdown)
