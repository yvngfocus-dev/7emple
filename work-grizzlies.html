The Challenge

What they
needed.

Grizzlies Zoetermeer is more than a softball and baseball club. It's a community built on sport, atmosphere, and connection. Every year they host the Grizzlies International Tournament. A weekend where teams from across the Netherlands and beyond converge on the field.

The goal wasn't just to look back. It was to grow. The club needed sports event content that would attract new international teams, convince sponsors with a professional image, and give the community something they'd share without being asked. Most event videos are forgotten within a week. Grizzlies needed one that would keep working long after the final out.

The brief: capture the energy, community, and emotion of the tournament in an aftermovie and photo series that could serve as a strategic asset across social media, the website, and sponsor pitches.

Our Approach

  • 01 On the field and off it. We didn't just film the sport. We filmed everything around it. The tension before a pitch, the laughter on the sideline, the moment a team celebrates together. Real moments, unscripted. That's what makes people share.
  • 02 A photo series with purpose. Alongside the aftermovie, we delivered a full tournament photo series focused on emotion, interaction, and the small details that make a club feel alive. Content built to work across socials, the website, and sponsor decks.
  • 03 Strategic asset. Built to keep working. The aftermovie was designed as the club's digital calling card. A two-minute answer to "what is the Grizzlies International Tournament?" that sponsors, new teams, and prospective members could find and feel immediately.

The Outcome

What we
delivered.

What started as a sports event aftermovie became a strategic asset the club builds on every year. The content didn't just recap the tournament. It put Grizzlies Zoetermeer on the map internationally. New teams from France, Germany, and England registered. Sponsors were quicker to commit. Members shared the video themselves without being prompted. The film created pride in the existing community and excitement in those who hadn't been there yet. That's the difference between content that looks good and content that actually scores.
